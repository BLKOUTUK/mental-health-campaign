1. Project Overview

"Voices in Relay" is a pilot podcast episode that introduces a bold proposal: £10.5 million in mental health reparations for Black queer men in the UK over five years. But this isn't a traditional podcast interview—it's a relay.

Six participants engage in 7-minute segments where each person answers questions from the previous participant, then transitions to interviewer for the next person. The relay continues until it returns to the anchor participant, creating a circular structure where everyone is both teacher and learner, both questioned and questioner.

"Without community, there is no liberation. This format makes that visible—we're not individuals speaking to an audience, we're a community thinking together."

Why This Matters

Black queer men in the UK are five times more likely to need mental health support than the general population, yet services aren't designed for us. The mental health reparations proposal offers a comprehensive response: culturally-specific therapy, peer support networks, employment programmes, and structural change across six sectors.

But big ideas need compelling communication. This podcast pilot creates a democratic space for dialogue—bringing together lived experience, clinical expertise, research evidence, commissioning knowledge, grassroots organising, and political will. The relay format isn't just innovative audio; it embodies the co-production values at the heart of the proposal.

2. The Relay Format: How It Works

Structure

  1. Introduction (5 minutes): Host Dr. Rob Berkeley sets context and explains the relay
  2. Six 7-minute segments: Each participant interviewed by the previous person
  3. Return to anchor: Final participant questions the first, completing the circle

Why Relay?

  • Time pressure demands clarity: 7 minutes forces participants to get to the point
  • Democratises expertise: Clinical psychologists and community organisers have equal voice
  • Creates collective intelligence: Understanding builds through dialogue, not monologue
  • Embodies the proposal's values: Co-production isn't just rhetoric—it's how we make the episode
